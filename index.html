About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at The George Washington University, graduating in May 2026. My research focuses on corporate debt, sovereign debt, and international capital flows. I study how capital-supply uncertainty, global financial conditions, and policy frameworks shape firm and sovereign financing decisions. My job market paper examines how mutual fund flow volatility influences corporate bond refinancing timing.

Research

  • Corporate Bond Refinancing Under Capital Supply Uncertainty
    Job Market Paper. Examines how mutual fund flow volatility affects refinancing timing, with strong effects for high-yield firms.
  • Push vs. Pull Factors in International Capital Flows
    Working paper with Grace Sun and Tomas Williams. Shows that interactions between global push and local pull factors significantly amplify flow volatility.
  • Sovereign Green Bonds
    Working paper. Documents four rationales for sovereign green bond issuance and identifies a 5 bps greenium on average, larger in EMs.
